April 2026 has been an exceptionally active month for AI research and development, marked by a rapid cadence of new model releases, significant paper submissions, and notable advancements in benchmark performance. ### Key Announcements by Organization

**OpenAI**

*   **GPT-5.5 Series:** OpenAI released a suite of GPT-5.5 models, including GPT-5.5 Instant, GPT-5.5 Pro, and GPT-5.5 Thinking variants, aiming for improved accuracy, clarity, and STEM question answering. GPT-5.5 Instant replaced GPT-5.3 Instant as the default model for ChatGPT. The GPT-5.5 series also includes Codex variants for agentic coding tasks. The company also doubled pricing for some GPT-5.4 models while introducing GPT-5.5.
*   **GPT-4o Retirement:** GPT-4o was fully retired from all ChatGPT plans after April 3, 2026.
*   **Advanced Account Security:** OpenAI rolled out advanced account security features for personal ChatGPT accounts to mitigate risks of unauthorized access.

**Google DeepMind**

*   **Gemma 4 Family:** Google DeepMind released the Gemma 4 family of open-weight multimodal models, available in various sizes (E2B, E4B, 26B A4B, and 31B) and featuring advanced reasoning and agentic workflows. These models support text, image, video, and audio input, with context windows up to 256K tokens.
*   **Gemini 3.1 Updates:** Gemini 3.1 received updates including real-time voice and image analysis capabilities. Gemini 3.1 Pro was noted for its multimodal and long-context capabilities.

**Anthropic**

*   **Claude Mythos Preview:** Anthropic confirmed the existence of Claude Mythos Preview, a highly capable frontier research model positioned above the Claude 4 family. Access is currently limited to an invitation-only consortium called Project Glasswing, focused on stress-testing safety envelopes.
*   **Claude Opus 4.7:** This model offers significant improvements in software engineering, instruction following, and vision capabilities. It also introduces new safeguards for detecting and blocking high-risk cybersecurity requests.
*   **Managed Agents and Claude Design:** Anthropic released Managed Agents for running long-running agent work and Claude Design, a collaborative visual creation tool.

**Meta**

*   **Muse Spark:** Meta unveiled Muse Spark, the first model from its new Meta Superintelligence Labs. It is a natively multimodal reasoning model with tool-use, visual chain-of-thought, and multi-agent orchestration capabilities. Muse Spark is being integrated into Meta's platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram, replacing existing Llama models.
*   **Contemplating Mode:** Meta also introduced "Contemplating Mode," which allows multiple agents to reason in parallel, aiming to compete with advanced reasoning modes of other frontier models.
