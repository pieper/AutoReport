## Key Announcements, Grouped by Organization

### The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA)

The TCGA program, a joint effort by the National Cancer Institute (NCI) and the National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI), continues to be a primary source for comprehensive multi-omic cancer data. Imaging data, including CT and MRI scans, are increasingly integrated and accessible through The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA) and the NCI Imaging Data Commons (IDC).

*   **TCGA Liver Hepatocellular Carcinoma (LIHC):** This collection provides matched clinical, genetic, and radiological data for hepatocellular carcinoma, enabling research into genotype-phenotype correlations.
*   **TCGA Kidney Cancer Datasets:** Multiple TCGA datasets focus on kidney cancers, including clear cell renal cell carcinoma (KIRC), kidney renal papillary cell carcinoma (KIRP), and a general kidney cancer dataset. These often include genomic and clinical data, with imaging data available through TCIA.

### The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA)

TCIA serves as a repository for a vast amount of cancer-related medical imaging data, often linked to TCGA and other cancer research projects.

*   **HCC-TACE-SEG:** This dataset contains pre- and post-procedure CT imaging studies of 105 HCC patients who underwent transarterial chemoembolization (TACE), with semi-automatic segmentations of liver, tumors, and blood vessels.
*   **C4KC-KiTS:** This collection includes CT scans and segmentations from the Kidney and Kidney Tumor Segmentation Challenge (KiTS19), featuring 210 patients with manual segmentations of kidneys and tumors. This dataset is particularly valuable for segmentation algorithm development.

### Duke University

*   **Duke Liver Dataset (DLDS):** This dataset comprises 2146 abdominal MRI series from 105 patients, many with cirrhotic features, and includes human-derived liver segmentations for 310 series. It is well-suited for developing automated series classification and liver segmentation models. It can be supplemented with datasets like CHAOS or LiTS for larger sample sizes and cross-modality training.

### Stanford University (AIMI)

The Stanford Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine and Imaging (AIMI) provides annotated medical data for research.

*   **Merlin Abdominal CT:** This dataset consists of 25,494 abdominal CT scans from 18,317 patients, each paired with its corresponding radiology report. While not specifically focused on liver or kidney, its comprehensive nature for abdominal imaging is notable.

### Human Organ Atlas (HOA) Hub

*   **Human Organ Atlas (HOA):** Building on an initial release in 2023, the HOA provides an open-access 3D portal to explore human organs, including the kidneys and liver, at near-cellular resolution using Hierarchical Phase-Contrast Tomography (HiP-CT). This is a significant advancement for anatomical and disease research at a microscopic level.

### Other Notable Datasets

*   **LiMT (Multi-task Liver Image Benchmark Dataset):** This dataset includes CT volumes from 150 cases with various liver diseases and normal cases, carefully annotated by clinicians.
*   **LiverHccSeg:** Provides liver and HCC tumor segmentations on multiphasic contrast-enhanced MRI from two radiologists, including an analysis of inter-rater agreement. It contains 17 cases for liver segmentation and 14 for HCC tumor segmentation.
*   **ATLAS Dataset:** This dataset offers 90 liver-focused contrast-enhanced MRI scans of patients with unresectable HCC, along with corresponding liver and tumor segmentation masks. It is noted as the first public dataset providing CE-MRI of HCC with annotations.
*   **Open Kidney Dataset:** This dataset includes over 500 2D B-mode abdominal ultrasound images with fine-grained polygon annotations for four classes, generated by expert sonographers. It is released under a CC BY-NC-SA license, meaning it's for non-commercial use only.

## Possible Clinical Relevance

These datasets are crucial for advancing diagnostic accuracy, treatment planning, and the development of new therapeutic strategies for liver and kidney diseases.

*   **Improved Cancer Detection and Segmentation:** Datasets like LiTS, KiTS19, HCC-TACE-SEG, LiverHccSeg, and ATLAS provide rich annotations for training AI models to detect, segment, and characterize tumors in the liver and kidneys.
